Denver Traffic Safety Analysis: Neighborhood Accident Hotspots

Python • Pandas • Plotly • Data Visualization | February 2026

Comprehensive analysis of 12 years of Denver traffic accident data (266,895 records across 78 neighborhoods) to identify safety patterns and inform infrastructure decisions. Combined statistical analysis with interactive geospatial visualization to distinguish between high-volume and genuinely dangerous areas.

  • Identified Central Park as having 45 fatalities—nearly double any other neighborhood—linked to wide, high-speed arterial road design
  • Discovered "hidden danger" neighborhoods with disproportionately high severity rates despite lower traffic volumes
  • Quantified permanent COVID-19 impact: most neighborhoods never recovered to pre-pandemic accident levels
  • Created interactive visualizations revealing Colfax Avenue as Denver's most dangerous corridor
